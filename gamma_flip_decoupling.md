### 1. The Positive Gamma Regime (Above Threshold)
When the aggregate market GEX is heavily positive, market makers are long gamma. This occurs when the underlying asset is trading comfortably above major option open interest clusters (such as the \$750.00 SPY Call Wall).
* **The Hedging Rule**: Market makers must buy underlying stock when the price declines, and sell underlying stock when the price rises. They trade **counter to the prevailing trend**.
* **Systemic Impact**: This acts as an automated stabilizing drag on the market, compressing realized volatility, creating tight visual ranges, and turning heavy institutional strike positions into impenetrable upward or downward ceilings.

### 2. The Negative Gamma Regime (Below Threshold)
When the underlying spot price falls below major option insulation layers, aggregate market GEX flips into negative territory. Market makers are now short gamma.
* **The Hedging Rule**: Market makers must sell underlying stock when the price declines, and buy underlying stock when the price rises. They trade **in the direct direction of the prevailing trend**.
* **Systemic Impact**: This acts as a massive, non-discretionary volatility accelerator. Because HFT market-making networks are forced to aggressively sell into a falling market to maintain delta neutrality, they trigger violent downward spirals, cascade retail stop-losses, and systematically flush out under-capitalized accounts.
